Week of January 18-24

Monday-Tuesday (Jan 18-19): Lingering snow showers taper off. Additional 2-4" possible at northern areas (Baker, Stevens).

Snow Pattern Summary

We're in an active northwest flow pattern that favors northern and central Cascade ski areas. Mt. Baker continues to be the snowfall king, but Stevens Pass and Crystal Mountain are also benefiting. Snoqualmie and White Pass seeing decent amounts but less than areas further north.

Longer Range (Late January): Pattern may shift slightly, but overall active weather expected to continue through month end. Good news for building the base!

ğŸ¿ My Recommendations

Best Choice This Weekend: Mt. Baker

If you can make the drive, Baker is going to be absolutely epic. 1-2 feet of cold smoke powder with good snow quality. Saturday morning will offer the best visibility before the heavy snow moves in Saturday afternoon.

Runner-Up: Stevens Pass

More accessible for Seattle-area folks and still getting solid snowfall. Saturday is your day here - get there early for first tracks on 6-10" of new snow.

South Sound Choice: Crystal Mountain

Good snowfall amounts and excellent terrain. Watch the upper mountain winds Sunday - could affect lift operations on the high alpine chairs.

Quick Trip: Snoqualmie

While not getting the huge totals, still a fun weekend with fresh snow and easiest access from Seattle. Great for a quick morning or afternoon session.
